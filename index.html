Who am I?

Born in Mexico, I moved to France at the age of 18 to pursue my passion for building health technologies that can make a real difference for patients and clinicians.

I have always been fascinated by the potential of technology in the medical field. Driven by the idea of creating practical impact, I focus on computer vision and medical imaging pipelines, from robust acquisition-aware processing to validated results that can support decision-making.

I first explored healthcare through the bachelor's program TechSan, then moved toward software engineering and medical imaging, where I could build tools with direct clinical impact. I later specialized in image processing and computer vision.

Today, I build robust C++ and Python solutions for medical imaging, combining computer vision, optimization, and data-driven approaches. I enjoy translating complex technical problems into practical tools that can be tested, validated, and adopted by teams. You can explore my work in the projects section.
